How to Get Alpha Boost, Custom Bodies, Decals, Wheels and Explosions in Rocket League (2026)

Bakkboard's Cosmetics Swap lets you replace any car body's decal, wheels or boost trail — including the legendary Alpha Boost — without running anything inside the game. Originals are backed up automatically and restored with a single click.

Browsing and applying custom bodies, decals, wheels, boosts and explosions in Bakkboard.

How cosmetics swap works

Rocket League stores car body and cosmetic assets as UPK files inside its game directory. Bakkboard reads these files, lets you pick a replacement, patches the file with the new asset, and stores the original in a local backup folder. The game then loads the patched file when you launch a match — no runtime injection, no DLL hooking, nothing running inside RocketLeague.exe.

Because the swap happens at the file level, not in memory, it works with any launcher (Steam or Epic Games) and does not require administrator privileges once the files are writable.

What you can swap

  • Boosts — swap your boost trail to any boost in the game, including the Alpha Boost (the gold rush trail that has become the most sought-after cosmetic in Rocket League history). Apply it to any car without owning it.
  • Decals — apply any decal to any car body, including designs that the game normally restricts to specific bodies.
  • Wheels — swap to any wheel set on any car, including cross-body wheels that the game normally locks to specific models.

Changes apply per car body, so you can have a different body, decal, wheel and explosion loadout on Octane, Fennec, Dominus and any other body simultaneously.

Alpha Boost — why everyone wants it

The Alpha Boost — officially called Gold Rush — was given exclusively to players who participated in the Rocket League alpha in 2014. It has never been available for purchase and cannot be traded for in the current item system. For most players it is completely out of reach in normal gameplay.

With Bakkboard's cosmetics swap, you can apply the Alpha Boost UPK file locally so it appears in your game — only visible to you, not to other players online. It is the same approach BakkesMod used for years.

Restoring originals — one click

Every time Bakkboard patches a UPK file, it automatically backs up the original to %LocalAppData%\Bakkboard\backups. Restoring is a single button press — Bakkboard replaces the patched file with the backed-up original, and the next game launch loads the stock asset as if nothing was changed.

You can restore individual items or all cosmetics at once.

No risk to your account

Bakkboard's approach to cosmetics is identical in principle to changing a texture pack in any offline game — it swaps files on disk before the game process starts. The swap is purely local and invisible to Psyonix's servers. You are not modifying game memory, not injecting code and not communicating with any external service to enable the swap.

Restore your originals before any competitive session if you want to play it safe — one click is all it takes.

Step by step

  1. Open Bakkboard and go to the Cosmetics tab.
  2. Select the car body you want to customize from the list.
  3. Browse the available bodies, decals, wheels, boosts or explosions and click Apply on the one you want.
  4. Launch Rocket League — your car will use the swapped cosmetic.
  5. To restore the original, click Restore on that item and relaunch the game.

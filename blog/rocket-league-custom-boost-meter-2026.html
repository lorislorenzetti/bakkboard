How to Get a Custom Boost Meter in Rocket League (2026)

The boost meter is one of the most-watched elements on the Rocket League HUD — you glance at it hundreds of times per match. Bakkboard lets you replace its textures with any custom design, so it looks exactly the way you want.

Applying a custom boost meter skin from Bakkboard's Boost Meter panel.

What a custom boost meter skin does

Bakkboard lets you replace the entire boost meter design with a completely different look. You can choose from the pre-included packs that come with Bakkboard, or import your own design for a fully personalised result.

How to apply a custom boost meter skin

  1. Open Bakkboard and go to the Boost Meter tab.
  2. Browse the pre-included packs — each one replaces all relevant boost bar textures as a coordinated set.
  3. To use your own artwork, click Import and select your texture file.
  4. Select the pack or file you want and click Apply.
  5. Relaunch Rocket League. Your custom boost meter is now active.

Switching between different designs is just as quick — select a new pack, apply, relaunch. No reinstall or file hunting required.

Restore the original boost meter in one click

Bakkboard backs up all original texture files before making any changes. To go back to the default boost meter at any time — before a tournament, when testing something, or just for a change — click Restore Original in the Boost Meter tab. The backup is applied instantly without touching any other part of your game installation.

If a Rocket League update overwrites your custom skin, you can re-apply your chosen pack from the same tab in seconds.

No ban risk

Bakkboard patches texture files on disk before the game launches. By the time Rocket League starts and Easy Anti-Cheat initialises, the files are already in place. There is nothing happening at runtime — no injection, no hooks, no memory access. Easy Anti-Cheat simply sees texture files, same as it always does.

This approach is consistent across every Bakkboard cosmetic feature, from decals and wheels to ball skins and boost meters. The original files are always preserved and restorable.

Rocket League Overlays in 2026 — Stats Bar, Rank Badge and More

Bakkboard brings four independent overlays to Rocket League: a session Stats Bar, a Rank Badge, a live Match Scoreboard and Magic Rank — all without touching the game process. Each one is positionable, scalable and opacity-adjustable.

Bakkboard overlays running during a live ranked match.

Stats Bar

The Stats Bar is a compact HUD overlay that shows your session performance at a glance: matches played, wins, losses, win rate, current streak and MMR delta. It sits on screen while you play and updates after every match.

It adapts automatically to the playlist you are currently playing. If you switch from 3v3 to 2v2, the Stats Bar switches its numbers to reflect that mode's session — no manual switching needed.

You can position it anywhere on any connected monitor, scale it up or down, and set its opacity. It is click-through when locked, so it never interferes with the game.

Rank Badge

The Rank Badge overlay shows your current rank, division and MMR as a clean floating badge. Like the Stats Bar, it tracks the active playlist automatically — switch from Duel to Doubles and it updates to show the right rank without any input from you.

The badge reads rank data from Tracker.gg and caches it locally, so it works even without an active internet connection after the initial lookup.

Match Scoreboard overlay

During an active match, the Match Scoreboard overlay shows both teams, the live score and rank icons for every player in the lobby. It is designed for players who want to see rank information on a second monitor without alt-tabbing or checking their phone.

It appears automatically when a match starts and hides when the match ends. Rank icons are always visible — there is no toggle to manage.

Magic Rank

Magic Rank is the most unique overlay: it places rank icons directly on the in-game scoreboard while you are playing, at the exact coordinates where BakkesMod's RocketStats used to show them. You see your opponents' and teammates' ranks without leaving the game view.

It requires a one-time calibration step — you open the in-game tab scoreboard and tell Bakkboard where to anchor the icons. After that it works automatically in every match.

Shared settings for all overlays

  • Position — snap to any corner or drag to a custom position. You can also drag overlays directly on screen while they are visible.
  • Scale — resize from the settings panel, no restart needed.
  • Opacity — reduce transparency so the overlay blends with your setup.
  • Monitor selection — choose which connected display the overlays appear on. Changing the monitor repositions all active overlays immediately.
  • Auto-hide when unfocused — optionally hide all overlays when Rocket League is not the active window, so they do not appear on your desktop or during screen share.

OBS and streaming

Each overlay has a dedicated OBS source URL that you can add as a browser source in OBS Studio. The overlay renders in OBS independently from your desktop, so you can show it on stream without it appearing on your physical display during play.

How to Apply Custom Decal Packs in Rocket League (2026)

Beyond swapping decals the game already ships with, Bakkboard's Custom Decals tab gives you a curated catalog of community-made decal packs — fully custom designs you can browse, download and apply to your car body in a couple of clicks.

Browsing and applying a custom decal pack from Bakkboard's Custom Decals catalog.

What a custom decal pack is

A custom decal pack is a fan-made design — not something that exists in Rocket League's own item system — packaged as a set of textures for a specific car body. Bakkboard's Custom Decals tab hosts a growing, curated catalog of these packs, each one verified before it is published so it applies cleanly and looks right in game.

This is different from swapping to an existing in-game decal (covered by Bakkboard's general Cosmetics Swap): here you are applying designs that were never part of the game at all. Like every other cosmetic change in Bakkboard, it is entirely local — only you see it, every other player in the match still sees your normal decal.

How to apply a custom decal pack

  1. Open Bakkboard and go to the Custom Decals tab.
  2. Browse the catalog — packs are organized by car body, so you only see designs made for the body you have selected.
  3. Preview a pack's thumbnail and pick the one you like.
  4. Click Download to fetch the pack, then Apply to patch it onto your car.
  5. Relaunch Rocket League. The custom decal is active immediately.

Packs are downloaded once and cached locally, so re-applying a pack you already downloaded is instant.

Car body compatibility

Every decal pack is built for a specific car body — the catalog only shows you packs that are compatible with the body you currently have selected, so you never end up trying to apply a design to the wrong car. Some packs are also skin-specific, meaning they target a particular official skin's texture set rather than the car's default paint job, and Bakkboard resolves this automatically when patching.

Restore the original decal in one click

Before patching anything, Bakkboard automatically backs up your car's original decal textures. If you want to go back to normal — for a tournament, to record a clean clip, or just to take a break from the custom look — click Restore Original in the Custom Decals tab. The backup is applied instantly, no reinstall needed.

No ban risk

Custom decal packs work through file patching at rest, exactly like every other cosmetic swap in Bakkboard. The texture files are replaced on disk before Rocket League launches — nothing is injected into the running game, no memory is hooked, and no process is manipulated. Easy Anti-Cheat only monitors what happens while the game is running, so it never observes any modification.

Because the catalog is curated and every pack is verified before publishing, you also avoid the trial-and-error of hunting for random decal files online and hoping they patch correctly.

How to Get Alpha Boost in Rocket League in 2026

Alpha Boost is the most iconic item in Rocket League history — and it has been unobtainable for years. BakkesMod made it possible to see it locally. Now BakkesMod is gone. Bakkboard is the replacement.

Applying a custom boost via Bakkboard's Cosmetics Swap panel.

BakkesMod is gone — and so is the easiest workaround

Alpha Boost — the golden boost trail awarded to Rocket League alpha testers in 2014 — has always been one of the rarest and most recognisable items in the game. For years, BakkesMod let players apply it locally as a visual override, without touching their actual inventory. It was one of the most popular uses of the plugin.

When Epic Games enabled Easy Anti-Cheat for Rocket League, BakkesMod stopped working. The DLL injection it relied on was exactly what the anti-cheat was designed to block. That workaround is gone for good.

Bakkboard does the same thing — applying Alpha Boost locally so you see it on your car — without any injection. A completely different approach that works in 2026.

What Bakkboard's cosmetics swap does

Bakkboard patches the UPK asset file for your chosen boost slot on disk — before the game launches. When Rocket League loads, it reads the patched file through its normal asset loading pipeline, just as it would read any other game file. The result: you see the boost you applied on your car.

This is a local-only visual change. It only affects what you see on your own screen. Other players online see whatever you have actually equipped in the garage — your real inventory is unchanged. You are not adding items to your account. You are not modifying game memory at runtime.

No ban risk

Easy Anti-Cheat scans for process injection and memory manipulation — techniques that modify the game while it is running. Bakkboard does neither. The file is patched at rest, the game reads it normally, and the anti-cheat never sees anything unusual. This is the same reason Bakkboard can swap car bodies, decals, wheels and explosions with zero ban risk: it works entirely outside the game process.

How to apply Alpha Boost — step by step

  1. Download and open Bakkboard.
  2. Go to the Cosmetics tab and select the Boost section.
  3. Choose a boost from your inventory as the replace slot — this is the item you will equip in the Rocket League garage after the swap.
  4. Find Alpha Boost (or any other boost) in the list and click Apply.
  5. Restart Rocket League, then equip the replace slot item in the garage.
  6. Enter a match or free play — the boost you applied will appear on your car.

To revert, click Remove in the Cosmetics tab and restart the game. Bakkboard keeps a backup of the original file and restores it instantly.
